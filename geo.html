1) Difference between intra-community and extra-community migration
Intra-community migration refers to the movement of individuals within the European Union (EU), where EU citizens have the right to live and work in any member state. Extra-community migration involves individuals from non-EU countries migrating to the EU, requiring legal documentation such as visas and work permits. Source: European Commission
2) Six aspects that migration impacts in the EU
Social and public services, the economy, employment, discrimination, legislation, media representation of immigrants.
3) Negative aspects of emigration on a donor country
Emigration can result in significant challenges for donor countries, including labor shortages, economic instability, and demographic imbalances. The departure of skilled professionals leads to a phenomenon known as "brain drain," which refers to the large-scale emigration of educated and highly skilled individuals. Source: OECD

6) Positive impact of emigration on a donor country
It alleviates unemployment by reducing the labor pool, resulting in increased incomes for other workers. Many emigrants send remittances, boosting the economy of the donor country. (source: https://www.rand.org/pubs/monograph_reports/MR244.html)
7) Positive and negative impacts of migration on a host country
Migration contributes positively to host countries by addressing labor shortages, increasing cultural diversity, and stimulating economic growth. However, it can also strain public services, create housing demand pressures, and contribute to social tensions if integration policies are inadequate. A well-managed migration policy can maximize economic benefits while minimizing social challenges.
